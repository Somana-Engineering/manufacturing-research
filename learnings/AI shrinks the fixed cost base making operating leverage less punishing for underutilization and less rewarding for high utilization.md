# AI shrinks the fixed cost base making operating leverage less punishing for underutilization and less rewarding for high utilization

Fixed labor is perhaps the single biggest design debt item in factory economics. The conventional framework treats it as irreducible — you need engineers, quality managers, maintenance planners regardless of volume. But GenAI directly substitutes for many of these functions: quality engineering (root cause analysis on defect data, SPC, FMEA, DOE), maintenance planning (predictive AI replaces calendar-based scheduling), process engineering (AI analyzes process data, suggests parameter changes, implements them), and management oversight (the coordination layer becomes partially redundant when AI handles diagnosis and optimization).

These costs are not only reducible — they can become variable (scale AI compute up and down) or even inversely correlated with volume (AI gets better with more data, so per-unit "fixed labor equivalent" cost decreases). Less operating leverage means less penalty for underutilization and less reward for high utilization. This changes the entire capacity planning calculus and makes smaller production runs more economically viable — directly relevant to the a16z "factory-is-the-product" thesis where the moat from scale narrows and the moat from process intelligence becomes relatively more important.
